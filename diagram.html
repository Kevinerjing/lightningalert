How to draw the Lewis diagram

Step 1

Draw two neutral lithium atoms, each with one dot. Draw one sulfur atom with six dots.

Step 2

Draw an arrow from each lithium electron to sulfur. Sulfur needs two more electrons to complete an octet.

Step 3

After transfer, write the ions in brackets: [Li]⁺ [ :S: ]²⁻ [Li]⁺.

Common mistakes

  • Using only one Li atom
  • Leaving dots around Li⁺
  • Giving sulfur only 6 dots instead of 8
  • Writing S⁻ instead of S²⁻
How to draw the Bohr-Rutherford diagram

Before transfer

Each Li atom has shells 2,1. Sulfur has shells 2,8,6.

Electron transfer

Show one outer electron moving from each Li atom to sulfur.

After transfer

Each Li becomes Li⁺ with only 2 electrons. Sulfur becomes S²⁻ with shells 2,8,8.

Teacher check list

  • 2 lithium atoms shown
  • 1 sulfur atom shown
  • 2 electron arrows shown
  • Final ion charges shown correctly
