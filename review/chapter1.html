1. What This Page Covers

Lesson Targets

  1. Classify physical and chemical properties.
  2. Count protons, neutrons, and electrons for atoms and ions.
  3. Draw Bohr-Rutherford and Lewis dot diagrams.
  4. Name and write ionic formulas, including multivalent metals and polyatomic ions.
  5. Name and write covalent formulas using prefixes.

Fast Test Strategy

  1. Decide whether the compound is ionic or covalent first.
  2. For ionic compounds, use charges and simplify ratios.
  3. For covalent molecules, use prefixes and do not simplify formulas.
  4. For ions, change electrons only; protons stay fixed.

Counting Rules

atomic number = protons

mass number = protons + neutrons

neutrons = mass number - atomic number

Neutral atoms have equal numbers of protons and electrons.

Terms That Get Mixed Up

TermMeaningTrap
IsotopeSame element, different number of neutrons.Mass changes, not identity.
IonAtom or group with a charge.Atoms become ions by gaining or losing electrons, not protons.
CationPositive ion.Usually formed by losing electrons.
AnionNegative ion.Usually formed by gaining electrons.
Example: F- has 9 protons and 10 electrons. Fluorine gained one electron.

3. Bohr-Rutherford and Lewis Diagrams

Bohr-Rutherford Steps

  1. Find protons from the atomic number.
  2. Find neutrons from mass number minus protons.
  3. Find electrons from the charge.
  4. Fill shells in order: 2, 8, 8, then 18.

Lewis Dot Steps

  1. Write the element symbol.
  2. Use the group number to find valence electrons.
  3. For groups 13-18, subtract 10.
  4. Place single dots on four sides before pairing.
ExampleExpected thinking
BromineGroup 17, so 7 valence electrons.
OxygenGroup 16, so 6 valence electrons.
Li+Lithium has lost its one valence electron, so no valence dot.
O2-Oxygen gained 2 electrons and has a full octet.

4. Ionic Compounds

Properties

Ionic compounds are formed by electrostatic attraction between a cation and an anion, usually a metal and a nonmetal.

  1. High melting and boiling points.
  2. Generally soluble in polar solvents.
  3. Do not conduct electricity as solids.
  4. Conduct electricity when molten or dissolved in water.
  5. Usually brittle.

Formula Method

  1. Write the metal first and nonmetal/polyatomic ion second.
  2. Write charges.
  3. Crisscross charges into subscripts.
  4. Simplify to the lowest whole-number ratio.

Ca2+ + Cl- → CaCl2

5. Covalent Compounds

Covalent compounds form between nonmetals. They usually have lower melting and boiling points than ionic compounds, low water solubility, and poor electrical conductivity.

NumberPrefixExample
1monocarbon monoxide, CO
2disulfur dioxide, SO2
3triphosphorus trichloride, PCl3
4tetradinitrogen tetroxide, N2O4
5-10penta, hexa, hepta, octa, nona, decasulfur hexafluoride, SF6
Important: covalent formulas are not simplified. Hydrogen peroxide is H2O2, not HO, because the formula shows the actual molecule.

Diatomic molecules to memorize: H2, O2, F2, Br2, I2, N2, Cl2; also P4 and S8.

7. Practice Tests

Test 1: Core Skills

  1. For chlorine-35, find protons, neutrons, and electrons.
  2. For Mg2+, how many electrons are present?
  3. Compare ion vs. isotope.
  4. Draw or describe Lewis dots for bromine, oxygen, and Li+.
  5. Classify: Al2O3, SO2, CaF2, N2O.
  6. Name SnCl4.
Answers
  1. 17 protons, 18 neutrons, 17 electrons.
  2. 10 electrons.
  3. Isotopes differ in neutrons; ions differ in electrons and charge.
  4. Br has 7 valence electrons; O has 6; Li+ has no valence dot.
  5. Ionic, covalent, ionic, covalent.
  6. Tin(IV) chloride.

Test 2: Naming and Formulas

  1. Write formulas: calcium chloride, aluminum phosphate, magnesium sulfide.
  2. Name: Fe2O3, Cu3P, KMnO4.
  3. Write formulas: cobalt(III) oxide, lead(II) nitride, beryllium hydroxide.
  4. Name: N2O4, PCl3, SF6.
  5. Why is AlPO4 not Al3(PO4)3?
Answers
  1. CaCl2, AlPO4, MgS.
  2. Iron(III) oxide; copper(I) phosphide; potassium permanganate.
  3. Co2O3, Pb3N2, Be(OH)2.
  4. Dinitrogen tetroxide; phosphorus trichloride; sulfur hexafluoride.
  5. Ionic formulas must be simplified; Al3+ and PO43- make a 1:1 ratio.
