1. Core Concepts

Arrhenius Acids and Bases

Acid: produces H⁺ in water. Example: HCl + H₂O → H₃O⁺ + Cl⁻.

Base: produces OH⁻ in water. Example: NaOH → Na⁺ + OH⁻.

Trap: a compound can be basic without ending in OH, such as hydrogen carbonate, because it can produce OH⁻ in water.

2. Neutralization and Acid Reactions

Neutralization

Acid + base → ionic salt + water.

HCl(aq) + LiOH(aq) → LiCl(aq) + H₂O(l)

Method: combine H⁺ and OH⁻ into water, then pair the remaining ions to make the salt.

Acids with Carbonates

Acid + carbonate → salt + carbon dioxide + water.

H₂SO₄ + CaCO₃ → CaSO₄ + CO₂ + H₂O

Trap: carbonate questions often ask for an observation: bubbles of CO₂.

pH Scale

pH < 7 acidic; pH = 7 neutral; pH > 7 basic.

The pH scale is logarithmic: a difference of 1 pH unit means a 10 times change.

Example: pH 2.3 to 6.7 is 10⁴⋅⁴ ≈ 25,000 times less acidic, so it became about 25,000 times more basic.

4. Acid Deposition and Applications

TopicWhat to remember
Natural rainAbout pH 5.6 because dissolved CO₂ forms carbonic acid.
Acid depositionBelow pH 5.6; caused by sulfur oxides and nitrogen oxides from fossil fuels.
EffectsDamages plants, leaches soil minerals, harms aquatic life, and damages buildings.
Pool pHGood range is about 7.2-7.8. If pH is 8.2, water is basic, cloudy, and chlorine works less well; add acid such as HCl to lower pH.

Practice Test 1: Easy-to-Miss Questions

Try these first without looking at the answers.

  1. Classify each as acid, base, or neutral: HNO₃(aq), KOH(aq), KNO₃(aq).
  2. Name H₂SO₄(aq), HI(aq), and Ba(OH)₂(aq).
  3. What ions are produced by an Arrhenius acid and an Arrhenius base in water?
  4. Predict and balance: HI(aq) + NaOH(aq) →
  5. Predict and balance: HBr(aq) + Ba(OH)₂(aq) →
  6. Phenolphthalein is added to a basic solution. What colour should it be?
  7. Blue litmus is placed in hydrochloric acid. What happens?
  8. A solution has pH 3. Is it acidic, neutral, or basic?
  9. What gas forms when an acid reacts with a carbonate?
  10. Why is rainwater naturally slightly acidic?
Answers and analysis: Test 1
  1. HNO₃ is acid; KOH is base; KNO₃ is neutral.
    Nitrates of Group 1 metals are salts and are usually neutral in this context.
  2. Sulfuric acid; hydroiodic acid; barium hydroxide.
  3. Acids produce H⁺; bases produce OH⁻.
    In water, H⁺ is often represented as H₃O⁺.
  4. HI + NaOH → NaI + H₂O
    Neutralization makes salt plus water.
  5. 2HBr + Ba(OH)₂ → BaBr₂ + 2H₂O
    Two OH groups need two H atoms from acid.
  6. Pink.
  7. It turns red.
  8. Acidic.
  9. Carbon dioxide, CO₂.
  10. Dissolved CO₂ forms carbonic acid, H₂CO₃.

Practice Test 2: More Tricky Mixed Questions

  1. Predict and balance: H₃PO₄(aq) + Ca(OH)₂(aq) →
  2. Predict and balance: HNO₃(aq) + Al(OH)₃(s) →
  3. Write the balanced reaction for sulfuric acid with calcium carbonate.
  4. A pH changes from 2.3 to 6.7. About how many times more basic did it become?
  5. A lemon turns phenolphthalein from pink to colourless. Explain why.
  6. Universal indicator: A is yellow, B is blue, C is peach. Which is likely ammonia and which is likely vinegar?
  7. Would sugar water conduct electricity well? Explain.
  8. Why can lemon water reduce fishy seafood odour caused by amines?
  9. Pool water has pH 8.2. Is it acidic or basic, and how could you lower the pH?
  10. Why does a limestone lake resist acidification better than a granite lake?
Answers and analysis: Test 2
  1. 2H₃PO₄ + 3Ca(OH)₂ → Ca₃(PO₄)₂ + 6H₂O
    Calcium is 2+ and phosphate is 3-, so the salt is Ca₃(PO₄)₂.
  2. 3HNO₃ + Al(OH)₃ → Al(NO₃)₃ + 3H₂O
    Aluminum is 3+, so three nitrate ions are needed.
  3. H₂SO₄ + CaCO₃ → CaSO₄ + CO₂ + H₂O
    Acid + carbonate produces salt, CO₂, and water.
  4. About 25,000 times more basic.
    The pH difference is 4.4, so the change is 10⁴⋅⁴.
  5. Lemon juice is acidic, and phenolphthalein is colourless in acid.
  6. Ammonia is B; vinegar is A.
    Blue indicates basic; yellow indicates acidic on the provided chart.
  7. No, or very poorly.
    Sugar dissolves as molecules, not ions.
  8. Lemon water is acidic and neutralizes basic amines.
  9. Basic; add acid such as HCl/muriatic acid to lower pH.
  10. Limestone contains CaCO₃, which reacts with and neutralizes acid.

Homework-Specific High-Risk Points

Q1-Q2: Acid, Base, or Neutral

Acids: HNO₃, H₂SO₄, HI, H₃PO₄, HBr.

Bases: KOH, Ba(OH)₂, LiOH, KHCO₃, Ca(HCO₃)₂, Fe(OH)₃.

Neutral: KNO₃.

Q5: Universal Indicator Bottles

A is yellow, B is blue, C is peach.

Ammonia: B, because blue indicates a base.

Vinegar: A, because yellow indicates an acid near pH 6-8 on the provided chart.

Third bottle: C is not uniquely known; it is acidic, but many acidic cleaning fluids could give peach.

Safety: do not pour an unknown cleaning fluid down the drain just from indicator colour.
