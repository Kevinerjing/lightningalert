2. Conservation of Mass and State Symbols

Law of Conservation of Mass

In a chemical reaction, the total mass of the reactants equals the total mass of the products. Atoms cannot be created or destroyed; they are rearranged.

Zn(s) + CuSO4(aq) → ZnSO4(aq) + Cu(s)

State Symbols

SymbolMeaning
(s)solid
(l)liquid or molten
(g)gas
(aq)aqueous: dissolved in water
Trap: NaCl(aq) means salt dissolved in water, not liquid salt.

3. Balancing Chemical Reactions

Golden rule: change coefficients only. Never change subscripts.

Fe + O2 → Fe2O3

Balanced: 4Fe + 3O2 → 2Fe2O3

RuleWhy it matters
Only add coefficients.Changing subscripts changes the substance.
Balance non-hydrogen and non-oxygen first.Hydrogen and oxygen often appear in multiple compounds.
Balance unchanged polyatomic ions as a unit.SO4 can be counted as one group if it appears unchanged on both sides.
Use simplest whole-number coefficients.Do not leave fractions or reducible coefficients.
If it cannot balance, check formulas.The skeleton equation may have a crisscrossing mistake.

  1. Use common charges for multivalent metals unless the question says otherwise.
  2. Remember diatomic elements when they appear alone: H2, N2, O2, F2, Cl2, Br2, I2.
  3. Double displacement only proceeds in this unit when a precipitate, water, or gas forms.

5. Combustion

Complete Combustion

Occurs with sufficient oxygen. Hydrocarbons produce carbon dioxide, water, and energy.

CH4 + 2O2 → CO2 + 2H2O + energy

Flame is usually blue with little smoke.

Incomplete Combustion

Occurs with insufficient oxygen. Possible products include carbon dioxide, carbon monoxide, water, solid carbon, and energy.

Flame is usually yellow/orange and smokier.

Safety: carbon monoxide is colourless, odourless, tasteless, and dangerous because it reduces oxygen transport in blood.

6. Homework High-Risk Answers

Q1: Dissolved vs. Precipitate

AgNO3(aq) + NaCl(aq) → AgCl(s) + NaNO3(aq)

Reactants: silver nitrate and sodium chloride.

Products: silver chloride and sodium nitrate.

Dissolved compounds: silver nitrate, sodium chloride, sodium nitrate.

Precipitate: silver chloride.

Q6: Conservation of Mass

Propane mass = 61.8 - 50.0 = 11.8 g

Product mass = 104.8 - 50.0 = 54.8 g

Oxygen consumed = 54.8 - 11.8 = 43.0 g O2

Trap: subtract the container mass before comparing reactant and product masses.

Test 1: Balance and Classify

  1. __K2O → __K + __O2
  2. __AlCl3 + __Na2CO3 → __Al2(CO3)3 + __NaCl
  3. Write and balance: magnesium reacts with oxygen.
  4. Write and balance complete combustion of propane.
  5. Classify: 2H2O → 2H2 + O2
Answers
  1. 2K2O → 4K + O2
  2. 2AlCl3 + 3Na2CO3 → Al2(CO3)3 + 6NaCl
  3. 2Mg + O2 → 2MgO
  4. C3H8 + 5O2 → 3CO2 + 4H2O
