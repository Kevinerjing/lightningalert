Grade 10 Math

Geometry Skill Map

Use this page to train one habit: identify the skill before solving. Kevin should first decide the type of question, then follow the matching steps.

Fast Question Check

  • What is the question asking me to find?
  • Do I see two points, a point and a line, or two lines?
  • Does the question say shortest distance or perpendicular?
  • What skill type is this?
  • What is my first step?

Main Goal

Kevin does not need more random practice first. He needs a skill map. When he sees a problem, he should say: "This is a two-point distance question" or "This is a shortest distance from a point to a line question."

Eric can use this page as a coaching guide and ask only two questions first: What type is this? and What is the first step?

How to Train Kevin

1. Name the skill first

Before solving, Kevin must say the type out loud or write it down.

2. Follow the steps

Do not jump to formulas. Start from the matching procedure for that skill.

3. Compare similar skills

Train him to tell the difference between two-point distance, point-to-line distance, and parallel-line distance.
