How to read the three diagrams

Bohr-Rutherford Diagram

  • Shows the nucleus and the electron shells.
  • The nucleus includes the number of protons and neutrons.
  • Use it when learning full atomic structure.
  • Ion versions show gained or lost electrons and the final charge.

Simplified Bohr Diagram

  • Shows the nucleus with protons and neutrons plus right-facing shell arcs.
  • Instead of drawing every electron, it shows shell counts like 2e⁻, 8e⁻, 1e⁻.
  • The numbers sit on the shell area, like common classroom notes.
  • Often used in Grade 9 chemistry worksheets and teacher diagrams.

Lewis Dot Diagram

  • Shows only the valence electrons.
  • Place one dot on each side before pairing.
  • Used to predict bonding and common ion charge.
  • For carbon, the correct Lewis diagram has 4 single dots.

Quick rules

  • Atomic number = number of protons.
  • Neutral atom: protons = electrons.
  • Ion: electrons change, protons stay the same.
  • Valence electrons = electrons in the outer shell.
